1) Basic regularity and derivative formulas
* For every σ>0\sigma>0, V(σ,⋅)∈C∞(R)V(\sigma,\cdot)\in C^\infty(\mathbb{R}) and V∈C∞((0,∞)×R)V\in C^\infty((0,\infty)\times\mathbb{R}).

* Weak/strong derivatives exist and are given by convolution:

∂σV(σ,⋅)=(∂σPσ)∗ψ,∂tV(σ,⋅)=(∂tPσ)∗ψ,\partial_\sigma V(\sigma,\cdot)=(\partial_\sigma P_\sigma)*\psi,\qquad \partial_t V(\sigma,\cdot)=(\partial_t P_\sigma)*\psi,
with
∂σPσ(t)=1π t2−σ2(σ2+t2)2,∂tPσ(t)=−2π σt(σ2+t2)2.\partial_\sigma P_\sigma(t)=\frac{1}{\pi}\,\frac{t^2-\sigma^2}{(\sigma^2+t^2)^2},\qquad \partial_t P_\sigma(t)=-\frac{2}{\pi}\,\frac{\sigma t}{(\sigma^2+t^2)^2}.
Both (σ,t)↦∂σV(σ,t)(\sigma,t)\mapsto \partial_\sigma V(\sigma,t) and (σ,t)↦∂tV(σ,t)(\sigma,t)\mapsto \partial_t V(\sigma,t) are Borel measurable.

∥∂σPσ∥L1(R)=∥∂tPσ∥L1(R)=2πσ.\|\partial_\sigma P_\sigma\|_{L^1(\mathbb{R})}=\|\partial_t P_\sigma\|_{L^1(\mathbb{R})}=\frac{2}{\pi\sigma}.

(These pointwise-in-σ\sigma bounds are sharp in homogeneity; for integrated L2L^2 control you should use §2 below.)

3) Whitney geometry and scale invariance
For an interval I=I(t0,L):=[t0−L,t0+L]I=I(t_0,L):=[t_0-L,t_0+L] and α≥1\alpha\ge 1, set the Whitney box
Qα(I)={(σ,t): t∈I, 0<σ<αL}.Q_\alpha(I)=\{(\sigma,t):\,t\in I,\ 0<\sigma<\alpha L\}.
Two scale-invariant controls are routinely useful.
3a) Local box control for arbitrary ψ∈L2\psi\in L^2 (localized mass)
There exist absolute constants c=c(α)>1c=c(\alpha)>1, C=C(α)C=C(\alpha) such that
∬Qα(I)∣∇V∣2 σ dσ dt  ≤  C(α) ∫cI∣ψ(t)∣2 dt.\iint_{Q_\alpha(I)} |\nabla V|^2\,\sigma\,d\sigma\,dt \;\le\; C(\alpha)\,\int_{cI} |\psi(t)|^2\,dt .
(Proof sketch: split ψ=ψ 1cI+ψ 1(cI)c\psi=\psi\,\mathbf{1}_{cI}+\psi\,\mathbf{1}_{(cI)^c}; the near part is handled by the L2L^2 area identity restricted to the box, the far part by off-support decay of PσP_\sigma and its derivatives plus Schur/Young; constants depend only on α\alpha.)
3b) Uniform box control for scaled windows (exact linear scaling in ∣I∣|I|)
Fix a base window ψ0∈L2(R)\psi_0\in L^2(\mathbb{R}) (e.g. compact support, even). For each I=I(t0,L)I=I(t_0,L) define the scale-translate
ψI(t):=ψ0 ⁣(t−t0L),VI(σ,t)=Pσ∗ψI(t).\psi_I(t):=\psi_0\!\left(\frac{t-t_0}{L}\right),\qquad V_I(\sigma,t)=P_\sigma * \psi_I(t).

4) Dilation rule (helpful for change-of-variables)
Let Sλ(σ,t)=(λσ,λt)S_\lambda(\sigma,t)=(\lambda\sigma,\lambda t). Then
(S_\lambda)^\!(\,\sigma\,d\sigma\,dt)=\lambda\;\sigma\,d\sigma\,dt,
and if ψλ(t):=ψ(t/λ)\psi_\lambda(t):=\psi(t/\lambda) then
Vλ(σ,t):=(Pσ∗ψλ)(t)=V ⁣(σλ,tλ).V_\lambda(\sigma,t):=(P_\sigma * \psi_\lambda)(t)=V\!\left(\frac{\sigma}{\lambda},\frac{t}{\lambda}\right).

What you can take away:
   * The Poisson extension is as regular as you need inside {σ>0}\{\sigma>0\}; the derivatives are measurable and given by explicit convolution kernels.

   * The weighted area norms of ∂σV\partial_\sigma V and ∂tV\partial_t V are exactly 14∥ψ∥22\tfrac14\|\psi\|_2^2 each; together they give 12∥ψ∥22\tfrac12\|\psi\|_2^2.

   * In Whitney geometry, the energy in Qα(I)Q_\alpha(I) is controlled either by the local L2L^2 mass of ψ\psi near II (3a), or—crucially for fixed windows scaled to II—by a universal linear bound ≲∣I∣\lesssim |I| with a constant depending only on the window shape (3b).
These are the “scale-invariant” L2L^2 controls you need for pairing/plateau arguments and for turning box-energy budgets into clean inequalities.
